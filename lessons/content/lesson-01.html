Before you give charity.

What This Means for You

This hadith teaches us a powerful truth: Allah looks at your heart before He looks at your actions.

If you do something to please Allah ‚Äî even if it is small ‚Äî it has real value.
But if you do something good just to be seen, praised, or admired, then that is all you get from it.

Because this lesson is so important, many great scholars of Islam started their books with this hadith. They wanted people to understand one thing before anything else:

If the intention is wrong, the action does not matter.

One famous scholar even placed this hadith at the very beginning of his book to send a clear message: an action done without sincerity has no real benefit ‚Äî not in this life, and not in the next.

So remember this:

Before you do anything, pause for one second and check your heart.

When you go to pray, ask yourself:
"Am I praying for Allah or because people are watching?"

When you give charity, ask yourself:
"Am I giving to please Allah, or to be noticed and praised?"

When you learn the Qur'an, ask yourself:
"Am I learning to show off what I know, or to get closer to Allah?"

When you help someone or do anything that looks good on the outside, always ask:
"Who am I really doing this for?"

If the answer is Allah, even small actions become huge.
If the answer is people, the reward stops with them.

Your intention is what turns normal actions into worship.
