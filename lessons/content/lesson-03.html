Lesson 3: Understanding the Beliefs of Islam

Imagine building a house. Before you build the walls, decorate the rooms, or paint anything, you need something strong underneath: a foundation. Without a solid foundation, the house will crack, lean, or even collapse. Your actions in Islam are like that house, and your belief is the foundation.

In Islam, we have two major parts: belief (what you believe in your heart) and actions (what you do with your body). Both are important, but belief comes first. You can pray five times a day, fast during Ramadan, give charity, and be kind to people‚Äîbut if your belief is weak or incorrect, those actions may not be accepted by Allah. That's why understanding correct belief matters so much.

Your belief is the foundation and pillars of your life as a Muslim.

Think about it this way: If someone prays but doesn't believe Allah is watching, or fasts but doesn't believe in the Day of Judgment, then what's the point? Allah says in the Qur'an that if a person rejects faith, their deeds can become worthless in the Hereafter‚Äîeven if they looked good on the outside.

Correct belief means knowing and accepting the truth about Allah, His Messenger Ô∑∫, the angels, the Books, the Day of Judgment, and divine decree (Al-Qadar). These are called the Six Pillars of Faith (Iman). We'll study each one in detail later, but for now, just know that they are the foundation you build everything else on.

Some people think belief is just a feeling or opinion‚Äîsomething you can make up based on what feels right to you. But Islam doesn't work that way. Your belief must come from what Allah revealed in the Qur'an and what the Prophet Muhammad Ô∑∫ taught in the Sunnah. You can't just believe whatever you want and call it Islam. That would be like building a house on sand instead of solid ground.

Here's the good news: When your belief is correct and your heart is sincere, even small actions become valuable. A smile, a kind word, removing something harmful from the road‚Äîthese can all be acts of worship if they are built on the right belief and done for Allah's sake.

So before we dive into the details of prayer, fasting, or any other practice, we need to make sure our foundation is strong. Belief comes first. Actions come second. And when both are correct, that's when your Islam becomes complete.
