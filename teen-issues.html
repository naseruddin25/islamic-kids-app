Social media can be a tool or a trap. It can connect you with knowledge and good people, or it can waste your time and distort your sense of self. The issue isn't the platform â€” it's how you use it.

Islam teaches you that your worth comes from Allah, not from likes, followers, or comments. When you base your identity on what other people think, you lose yourself. When you base it on your relationship with Allah, you stay grounded no matter what.

Start with: Short Lessons on Intention & Purpose

Reflect on these:

  • How much time do I spend on social media daily? Is it helping or hurting me?
  • Do I post or scroll to seek validation, or to genuinely connect and learn?
  • Am I comparing my real life to other people's highlight reels?
  • Do I feel anxious or inadequate after scrolling? What does that tell me?
  • Can I take a full day off from social media without feeling FOMO?

  • Am I patient with myself when I make mistakes?

Start with: Short Lessons on Purpose & Intention

  • What distractions keep me from being disciplined (phone, procrastination, laziness)?
  • Do I manage my time well, or do I always feel rushed?
  • Can I stick to a routine for 7 days straight?
