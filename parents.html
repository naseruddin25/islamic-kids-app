How to Use This Site with Your Teen

1. Let Them Explore Independently

Teens can browse Short Lessons or Teen Issues on their own. Independence builds ownership of their learning. Recommend starting with Foundations if they're new.

2. Use Lessons as Conversation Starters

If your teen wants to discuss what they learned, ask open-ended questions: "What stood out to you?" or "How does this apply to your life?" Avoid interrogating.

3. Quizzes Are Self-Check Tools, Not Tests

The quizzes help reinforce key points. They're not graded or tracked externally. Results can spark conversations about what resonated or what confused them.

4. Respect Their Pace

They might want to learn independently for weeks before discussing anything. That's healthy. Be available when they're ready to talk.

5. Model Curiosity

Show that learning is ongoing. Share what you're learning or revisiting about your own faith. This normalizes lifelong learning.

How Quiz Sharing Works

After completing a quiz, teens have the option to share their results via text message. Here's what you need to know:

Completely Optional

Sharing is never required. Teens can complete lessons and quizzes entirely privately if they prefer.

How It Works

If they choose to share, they can enter a parent/guardian phone number (stored locally on their device only). A pre-filled text message opens with their score and lesson topic.

Current Limitations

This is a static site with no backend. The sharing feature uses the device's default messaging app. It works best on mobile devices.

Use It to Start Conversations

If your teen shares a result, don't focus on the score. Ask what they learned, what surprised them, or if they have any questions.
